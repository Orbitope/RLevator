Background · the incumbents
Decades of clever rules — and where they run out

Elevator group control is a mature field. For seventy years the state of the art has been hand-engineered deterministic policies — rule systems and cost functions tuned by domain experts. They are robust, explainable, and very good. Our aim is not to replace them everywhere, but to beat them where their assumptions break: complex building configurations and shifting, mixed traffic.
